These terms are often confused, and I admit, it’s a little confusing looking from the outside. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Branding” means the entire process of creating a brand. <\/h3>\n\n\n\n

It’s a cooperation between the executive, marketing and design teams, working together to influence people’s perception. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Brand strategy” means the initial, strategic phase of branding.<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Brandstrat, as I like to call it, is the foundational process in which the intentions and future direction of an entity (person, or org) are clarified. Brand strategy is an investment in the meaning<\/strong> of your brand and how it will relate to your people beyond the obvious value proposition. A brand strategy is implemented over time across all touchpoints, modes and mediums.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Brand identity design means translating strategy.<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Identity design is where strategy is translated into a coherent system of assets, including a name, style and logo. Brand identity is a minimum requirement for doing business. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
