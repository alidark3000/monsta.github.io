These terms are often confused, and I admit, it’s a little confusing looking from the outside. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Branding” means the entire process of creating a brand. <\/h3>\n\n\n\n

It’s a cooperation between the executive, marketing and design teams, working together to influence people’s perception. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Brand strategy” means the initial, strategic phase of branding.<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Brandstrat, as I like to call it, is the foundational process in which the founding intentions and future direction of an entity (person, or org) are clarified. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Brand identity design means translating the strategy into concrete tactics.<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Identity design is where the strategy is translated into a coherent system of visuals and messaging. Just as identity design translates strategy into style, messaging is where the brand expresses itself in language. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Messaging is unfortunately often overlooked, and attempted by non-specialists in the design or marketing team. Always consult an expert copywriter or involve a seasoned brand strategist who also practices communication design. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
